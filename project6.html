A self-build housing project with a lightweight platform construction system.

Year  :  2017 - 2020

Hand sketches, as well as physical modelling, has become an integral part of the early design process. From city-scale massing to fine detail, both techniques allow for quick multiple tests of 2D and 3D views. Guildhall library model created an opportunity to recreate a detailed facade with plaster and MDF board while investigating the construction process.

Series of urban graphic prints examine geometry, light and shadow of well-known buildings by use of simple forms and vivid colours. Perspective graphics are based on photographs brought from trips to New York and Marseille.

International idea competition involved the submission of a proposal of the monastery for Sao Domingos da Batalha Fortress in Portugal. Challenging terrain, remaining ruins and spiritual characteristics of the project become crucial for the final design. Building refers to classical art examples in abstract architectural forms. The relative positioning of the rooms results in a hierarchical relationship between them (a reference to the Temple of Khonsu at Karnak). Rejection of the physical outside form of the project refers to the act of joining the community. Stairs leading down the building symbolises a spiritual journey of one's internal transformation.

Positioning of the rooms allows creating two large openings for natural light with the view of the sea. The uncommon shape of the room is created by the surfaces of two large spheres in near proximity which appears to almost touch each other. This concept refers to Michelangelo's 'The creation of Adam'. Chipping retrieved from the carving is used as interior material which naturally blends with surroundings.
