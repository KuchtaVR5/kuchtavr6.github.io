A House Between Two Trees:
A self-build project in Hackney.

Year  :  2021
Leading tutor  :  David Grandorge & Ted Swift
Institution  :  London Metropolitan University

Proposal for a self-build family house created in duet by Aleksandra Kuchta and Sam Newton. The site for the project is located on Balcorne Street, in London and it is a 6-meter wide elongated site with a tree on each end. The design focuses on the idea of rigorous simplification of the construction process to make it more accessible for the self-builder. The proposal follows the rhythm of existing terraced houses and offers a three-storey house with a similar footprint and aligned with the current street elevation.

On the ground floor, you can see services being placed left and two bedrooms on the right, divided with a long corridor which leads straight from the main entrance to the garden. In-built wardrobes along the division wall act as additional structural support and help with sound-proofing. The design includes a reverse floor plan with bedrooms on the ground floor and living on the first floor. Long space provides sunlight from the south and a great view of the garden. Double height in the dining area and large glazing makes it feel spacious. With a sloping ceiling, we imaged the space as an artist studio. The low sill in the kitchen window becomes a perfect spot to sit and enjoy the morning sun. The mezzanine on the top floor designed as a study has access to the roof terrace. The room can be converted into a third bedroom in the future if necessary.

In order to ensure the buildability potential of the proposal double stud wall has been introduced. This solution guarantees continuous insulation and airtightness. It also allows for an easy assembly process as all the elements can be easily erected and installed by three people. The spanning direction of the floor joists on each level as well as roof construction methodology supports the idea of simplified self-build design.
