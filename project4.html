The art foundation

Year  :  2019
Leading tutor  :  Jillian Jones, Ewan Stone & David Howarth
Institution  :  London Metropolitan University

The project explored the proposal for the art foundation in Mayfair, London. The space privately owned by art collector was designed to display and celebrate digital art. It focuses on the relationship between private and public elements of the building, to provide both: spaces for an art collector and his offices as well as exhibition rooms available for visitors. The proposal features a sequence of sculptural staircases in the central location which create an experience of the journey and enforces the order of exploration on each floor. Moreover, the location of the windows on the facade responds to the landing spots in order to generate unique viewpoints.

The main entrance to the building, as well as a detailed facade, overlook narrow and busy Avery Row. The facade overlooking Brook's Mews is a solid stone wall that interrupts the rhythm of elevations on this street. The corner of the triangular site was cut off on the ground floor to provide easy access. Digital art exhibition room requires an understanding of how art and space can affect multiple human senses. The design is focused on spatial rooms with good acoustics and relatively no daylight access to provide the best experience for viewers. The project is developed around the strong contrast between lightness and darkness.
