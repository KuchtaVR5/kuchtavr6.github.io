A room for art

Year  :  2018
Leading tutor  :  Jillian Jones, Ewan Stone & David Howarth
Institution  :  London Metropolitan University

The site for the project is located next to St James's Church in London. The main principle of the design was to combine a single gallery space open to the public with a private house for the art owner.

The Interior of the art gallery was carefully designed to accommodate a specific in its experience digital art. The proposal has explored two strongly contrasting spaces connected by a long narrow tunnel. From a standard room, which serves as a sitting area, visitors can enter tall dark space where digital art is exhibited. The screen positioned away from the back wall gives an illusion of deep darkness which makes it difficult to notice walls around it. The screen illuminates the room and peeks in through the tunnel to the sitting area.

The project's plan mirrors the layout of the church opposite. It creates a similar flow of people in the public art gallery and divides the yard into a more quiet enclosed park and a busy market with an entrance to the high street. On the first floor narrow balcony in the office area was placed in order to connect the art gallery connects with the rest of the house. It also demonstrates the importance of double-height spaces which provide multiple viewpoints.

The use of glass blocks panels as walls for the residential part of the building gives privacy and at the same time allows for a lot of daylight. However, at night it creates an impression of an illuminating cube. Moreover, the purpose of the spaces is reflected in the chosen materials: the art gallery is made out of dark concrete without windows keeping the space dark inside for film exhibition, whereas the private part of the house is made out of glass blocks forming a delicate looking shape.

The perspective section represents the art gallery tunnel in the middle of the ground floor which is wrapped around by the living spaces. Furthermore, rooms are separated from the outside by the glass blocks cloche.
