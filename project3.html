The Autoprogrettazione by Enzo Mari from 1974 provides a series of instruction manuals for numerous furniture pieces. The essence of the design guide is the use of standard woodcuts as well as a simplified assembly process which requires basic tools - hammer and nails; and common construction skills. The Spirit of Mariâ€™s book and the accessibility of the designs become a foundation for the next two self-build architectural projects. Building the chair allowed to experience in practice methods of building with timber and understand essential aspects of the construction. As the recreated furniture was using different section cuts than the original, the design has slightly changed into a more slender form. Order of assembly becomes crucial for the project as some elements attached too early would block the way and unable to continue with the next steps.
